## Where To Apply (Exact Websites)

### Primary Platforms (Use These Daily)

**1. LinkedIn (NOT Easy Apply)**
- URL: linkedin.com/jobs
- How: Search "GenAI Developer", "AI Engineer", "LLM Engineer", "Cloud AI" in Bangalore
- DO: Find the recruiter/hiring manager → message them → THEN apply on company career page
- DON'T: Click "Easy Apply" and move on. That's what 270 failed applications were.

**2. Instahyre**
- URL: instahyre.com
- How: Create profile, add all GenAI/AWS keywords. Recruiters reach out to YOU here.
- This platform has much less competition than Naukri/LinkedIn. Recruiters are active.

**3. Cutshort**
- URL: cutshort.io
- How: Create profile. Startup-focused platform. Good for GenAI roles at funded startups.
- Many AI startups in Bangalore hire through Cutshort.

**4. Wellfound (formerly AngelList)**
- URL: wellfound.com
- How: Create profile. Best for AI/ML startup roles. Salary ranges are visible upfront.
- Filter: Bangalore, ₹10-20 LPA, AI/ML/GenAI

**5. Company Career Pages (Direct Apply)**
- Go to target company's website → Careers page → Apply directly
- This bypasses aggregator noise. Your resume goes directly to their ATS.

## How To Apply (Step by Step)

### For Every Job, Follow This Process:

**Step 1**: Find the job on LinkedIn or company career page

**Step 2**: Find the recruiter or hiring manager on LinkedIn
- Search "[Company name] recruiter" or "[Company name] talent acquisition"
- Or search "[Company name] engineering manager AI"

**Step 3**: Send them this message BEFORE applying:

**Message to Recruiter:**
```
Hi [Name],

I'm a GenAI developer with 2 years of production experience 
on AWS Bedrock. I've built multi-agent systems with LangGraph, 
RAG pipelines, and a real-time voice bot — all shipped to 
production for enterprise clients.

I saw the [Role Title] opening at [Company] and I'm very 
interested. I've applied through your career page as well.

Would love to be considered. Happy to share more details.

Thanks,
Amit Rai
```

**Message to Employee (for referral):**
```
Hi [Name],

I noticed you work at [Company]. I'm a GenAI developer with 
production experience building AI systems on AWS Bedrock — 
RAG pipelines, multi-agent workflows, and voice bots.

I'm interested in the [Role Title] position. Would you be 
open to referring me? Happy to share my resume.

Thanks,
Amit
```

**Step 4**: Apply on the company career page directly (not through LinkedIn Easy Apply)

**Step 5**: Track it in a spreadsheet:
```
Company | Role | Applied Date | Recruiter Messaged | Referral Asked | Status | Follow-up Date
```

**Step 6**: If no response in 7 days, send one follow-up message:
```
Hi [Name], just following up on my message about the [Role] 
position. Still very interested. Let me know if you need 
any additional information. Thanks!
```

One follow-up only. If no response after that, move on.

## Salary Negotiation (When You Get an Offer)

**When asked "What's your expected CTC?":**
"Based on my production GenAI experience and the market rate for GenAI engineers in Bangalore, I'm targeting 14-16 LPA. I'm flexible based on the overall package and growth opportunities."

(Ask 14-16, they'll counter at 11-12, you negotiate to 12-13.)

**When you get an offer:**
- Never accept immediately. Ask for 2-3 days.
- If below target: "I was expecting closer to [X]. Is there flexibility?"
- For bond: "I have a transition obligation of 2L. Can this be covered as a signing bonus?"

**Never mention:**
- Current CTC (say "I'd prefer to discuss based on market rate and role expectations")
- Bond amount upfront (only when offer is in hand)
- Anything negative about Rapyder
